    About the Organization

    The Information Security Organization (ISO) is the sole cybersecurity-oriented student organization in De La Salle University, participating in CTF competitions and bringing awareness to network and information security-related topics and practices to the Lasallian community.

    ISO aims to provide skills-building, training, and opportunities to students from across the university who are interested in information security and to guide them toward becoming competent security professionals in the futre under the guidance of experts who are already in the field.

    Flagships and Plans

    Open Sessions

    Led by experienced members, faculty members, and industry experts, Open Sessions are exclusive and meant to hone the skills of members on topics usually covered by CTF competitions, such as digital forensics, cryptography, and the like. These sessions take the form of wargames and 101s.

    • Wargames are CTF or incident/attack simulations wherein participants are expected to get their hands dirty and gain firsthand experience with tools and concepts
    • 101s are a walkthrough-focused and theoretical approach to training, usually given by faculty members and professionals. They take the form of lecture-like sessions with discussions and Q&As in between in order to facilitate active learning.
